Various Sight-Seeing Spots!

Paris, France:

  • Paris is the capital and most populous city of France, serving as the centre and seat of government of the region.
    •
  • It is a major railway, highway, and air-transport hub served by two international airports.
    •
  • It is prominently recognized for its museums and architectural landmarks, including Louvre which remains the most visited museum in the world even amidst its closing due to the pandemic.
    •
  • The football club Paris Saint-Germain and the rugby union club Stade Francais are based in Paris while the 80,000 seat Stade de France is built for the 1998 FIFA World Cup.
    •
  • Paris hosts the annual French Open Grand Slam tennis tournament on the red clay of Roland Garros.
    •

    Maui, Hawaii:

  • Maui is part of the State of Hawaii and is the largest of Maui County’s four islands, and has the third-highest population of the Hawaiian Islands.
  • During periods of the reduced sea level, Maui’s smaller islands are joined together as a single island due to the shallowness of the channels between them.
    •
  • Maui is characterized by a two-season year, marked geographic differences in rainfall, high relative humidity, extensive cloud formations, and dominant trade-wind flow, which are specific to a loosely defined sub-region of the island.
    •
  • Maui is a leading whale-watching center in the Hawaiian Islands due to humpback whales wintering in the sheltered ‘Au’au Channel between the islands of Maui county.
    •
  • Maui is home to a large rainforest on the northeastern flanks of Haleakala, which serves as the drainage basin for the rest of the island.
    •


    Tokyo, Japan:

  • Tokyo is the political and economic center, the seat of the Emperor of Japan, and the national government of Japan.
  • The Greater Tokyo Area is the most populous metropolitan area in the world, with an estimated population of 37.468 million residents recorded in 2018.
    •
  • Tokyo is the largest urban economy in the world in terms of gross domestic product, and is categorized as Japan’s leading center of business and finance.
    •
  • Tokyo is an international center of research and development represented by several major universities such as the University of Tokyo.
    •
  • Tokyo has the world’s tallest tower, Tokyo Skytree, and the world’s largest underground flood water diversion facility, MAOUDC.
    •






    London, England:

  • London is the capital and largest city of England and the United Kingdom, exerting strong influence on its arts, commerce, education, and tourism.
  • London’s GDP makes it the biggest urban economy in Europe and one of the major financial centres in the world, only having the second highest number of ultra high net-worth individuals in Europe after Paris.
    •
  • London consists of Europe’s largest concentration of higher education institutions and is home to the most 5-star hotels of any city in the world.
    •
  • London has numerous museums, galleries, libraries, and sporting venues, and the London Underground is the oldest rapid transit in the world.
    •
  • London’s status as the capital of England, and later the United Kingdom, has never been granted or confirmed officially – by statute or in written form.
    •
